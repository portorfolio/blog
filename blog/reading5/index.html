The Internet Has Borders

revisit, site is down

Alive Internet Theory

This reminds me of a thought I typically have when I hear people say that the web will be overtaken with bots. I think to myself, "no way will people let that happen, they love being on the internet". The author makes an interesting proposition about thriving on the internet than just surviving. I think surviving on the internet means you're on it, but you're not really spending your time in the best/meaningful way for yourself. An example is scrolling mindlessly. When I accidentally fall into that, I end up feeling horrible. But when I'm on some cool artsy community made website it's like sunshine and rainbows.

I appreciate this blog for including some cool websites, like Melonland Forum and playhtml. I unintentionally spent the next half hour just scrolling through Melonland and even debated joining their Simmers guild. Places like this hold more meaning for me, and feels more trustworthy to interact with people who have the same interest as you.

Playhtml was also really cool. I think I was able to see other people's cursors on the screen, and there's draggable elements on screen which others can interact with as well. One of my favorite "games" on the site was minutes faces where ever minute can be colored only during that minute.

Some grids have multiple colors because there were several contributors for that specific minute. You're like indirectly interacting with others.

I Know a Place

I didn't think I would read a blog article about Roblox, and I also didn't expect it to make me feel kinda emotional???

I enjoyed reading about the author making a connection with The Foundation and Roblox. At the end, after getting through the recession, The Foundation managed to become what it was intended to be (a bunch of ugly houses). So now, it simply holds nostalgia and memories for the author. And who knows, Roblox may become that as well since they're not profiting. It's very sad to think about, and I wasn't aware that was the case. I feel like video games are one of the huge places on the internet where you can actually just sit back and hang out. Like, it carried my quarantine days. To know that it has the potential to not survive is disheartening. It goes back to the author's point, that things do disappear but experiences were still made.
